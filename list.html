Please understand that any one of these would be more useful and appreciated than any other gift in its price range, and I would use it at least until I moved out. Also, as much as I hate to admit it they can all pretty much relate in the fact that they have 88 working keys and would be great for practice and production. It’s the level of quality and functionality that defines the price of these instruments. For example, keyboard #1 is so great because you don’t need a fancy computer with hefty software to make it work. All you have to do is plug it into power and speakers and it immediately sounds and feels exactly like an actual fine grand piano: like seriously, this keyboard is the go-to for famous producers, concert tours, churches (including our church), and it has been used for tons of famous songs you hear on the radio. Also, the keys on #1 are weighted, which means they built physical mechanisms into the keyboard that make it feel like there is an actual hammer hitting a string when you press a key. It’s amazing really. On the other hand, the rest of the keyboards on this list are all midi controllers, which means they control software that is on a computer and they won’t work without a computer. These are still great options, and are used by many people for stage and production purposes, but the functionality is different. For #2, the keys are weighted and it is made of metal so it feels real just like #1, it also has a large array of controls, drum pads, and faders because it’s a midi controller, meant to control the computer software. #3 is essentially the same as #2 but it is made of plastic, has slightly less buttons and controls, and keys are not weighted like #1 and #2, so it feels barely comfortable but obviously fake. Finally, #4 is the bare minimum. It’s just a plain 88-key midi controller without all the buttons and fancy stuff.
